The respondentSo what?

Why would you want LLMs to predict vote intentions?

General-purpose inference

If LLMs can predict vote, it could mean they can infer other hard-to-measure variables from the same respondent profile.

Data imputation

Missing values, skipped questions, and incomplete batteries become candidates for model-assisted imputation.

Survey repair

Forgot to include a variable in your survey? A valid predictor could let you create an approximate version after the fact.

Unstructured text

Social media posts and other open-ended traces could become inputs for predicting vote intentions at scale.

05
The respondentLiterature

Silicon sampling

Aggregate success

LLMs can reproduce subgroup-level opinion distributions under demographic conditioning, but estimates remain sensitive to model choice, prompt wording, and target group.

Argyle et al. 2023 · Santurkar et al. 2023 · Bisbee et al. 2024

Individual-level limits

Group averages can look plausible while predictions for specific respondents remain weak. Persona prompts often flatten real variation, and multiparty, non-US settings are harder tests.

Röttger et al. 2023 · von der Heyde et al. 2025 · Taday Morocho et al. 2026

The respondentLLM comparison

Can frontier LLMs predict vote choice?

All four frontier LLMs cluster within a few accuracy points. We picked GPT-4o for the rest of the analysis: cost-efficient, no meaningful performance loss.

Multinomial regression: 55.9%

Same n = 1,000, same 24 features. gpt-4o 47.1%, multinomial 55.9%. McNemar p < 0.0001. 8.8 points.

13
Where the ceiling sitsHybrid approach

Fine-tuning? Hybrid priors?

Fine-tuning moves the needle a bit. The augmented prompt hybrid hits 53.5% - but it is not pure LLM: it feeds multinomial priors into the GPT-4o prompt. To recover the conventional-model gap you have to give the LLM the conventional-model output.

Where the ceiling sits
Off the shelf, the LLM ceiling sits below the conventional-model floor.

Where the errors liveAttribute sweep

What is the model actually predicting?

Vote choice 47%. But Quebec 91.9%, French language 90.8%, BQ vote 81.6%. The model is good at language and region. Vote choice is harder than language.
16
Where the errors liveProxy cues

Hide the proxy: the signal collapses

RCE hides the target cue and its nearby proxies; language, region, outdoor bundle. GPT-4o's lifestyle edge is almost entirely carried by redundant geo-linguistic signals.
17
Where the errors liveCHES distance

When wrong, models pull CPC voters to the centre

Arrow runs from true party position to where wrong predictions land on the left-right scale. CPC errors travel 3+ points leftward; BQ errors barely move. CHES party positions: Dassonneville & Koedam 2024 (CHES-Canada 2023).
18
Where the errors liveError overlap

Multinomial wins everywhere, except BQ

Multinomial outperforms GPT-4o on LPC (+21%), NDP (+13%), and CPC (+8%). GPT-4o's only edge: BQ voters (+16%), driven by its language detection.
19
Where the errors liveUS benchmark

US data, same gap.

3-category US sample, n = 1,000. gpt-4o 58.0% [54.8, 61.2]. Multinomial 63.3% [60.2, 66.3]. −5.3%. Different country, different party system. Same shape: the gap is method-level, not country-level.

What is this signal good for?

Not high-stakes individual decisions. But for low-stakes imputation, survey repair, and exploratory segmentation, off-the-shelf LLMs are already informative.
